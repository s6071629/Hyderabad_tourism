Here We Go,Hyderabad!
A city of stories, spice and soul.

Hyderabad, the capital of Telangana, is a vibrant city known for its rich history, iconic landmarks like the Charminar, and mouth-watering Hyderabadi biryani. Blending ancient traditions with modern tech, it's also home to Cyberabad, India's growing IT hub.

Birla Mandir

Perched atop a hill, Birla Mandir offers breathtaking views of Hyderabad and serene spiritual ambiance. Made of white marble, the temple is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara and attracts pilgrims and tourists seeking peace and panoramic cityscapes.

Charminar

An iconic symbol of Hyderabad, the Charminar stands in the heart of the old city with its striking arches and vibrant bazaars. Tourists flock here for its Indo-Islamic architecture, street shopping, and glimpses of local culture.

Ramoji Film City

Ramoji Film City is the world's largest integrated film studio complex, offering a magical blend of entertainment,cinema, and fantasy. Tourists can explore film sets, amusement parks,and live shows, making it a must-visit destination for families and movie lovers.

Golconda Fort

Once a majestic fortress of the Qutb Shahi dynasty, Golconda Fort is known for its impressive architecture, acoustic wonders, and panoramic views of the city. The evening light and sound show narrates its rich history, drawing heritage enthusiasts and tourists alike.

Hussain Sagar Lake

Built in 1562, this iconic heart-shaped lake links Hyderabad and Secunderabad. Known for its calm waters and the towering Buddha statue on an island, it's ideal for boat rides, peaceful walks, and catching beautiful sunsets.

Nehru Zoological Park

One of India's largest zoos, it spans 380 acres and houses over 1,500 species including tigers, elephants, and exotic birds. With safari rides, an aquarium, and picnic areas, it's a top family destination.

Situated beside Hussain Sagar, this city park is known for its musical fountains, laser light shows, and boat rides to the Buddha statue. It's a lively spot to unwind with family or enjoy a lakeside evening.

Falaknuma Palace, now a luxury Taj hotel, offers a glimpse into royal grandeur with its opulent interiors and lush gardens. Though access is limited, guided tours reveal the Nizam-era elegance that once welcomed royalty and dignitaries.

Salar Jung Museum

The Salar Jung Museum houses one of the largest one-man collections of art and antiques in the world. Visitors can explore artifacts from across the globe, including Persian carpets, European sculptures, and Mughal-era manuscripts.

Shilparamam is a cultural village that showcases traditional Indian crafts, folk art, and festivals. Tourists can shop for handmade goods and experience rural charm in the midst of the city.
